How passives work

Tiers (added in the Feybreak update) show how strong a passive is: +3 is the best standard tier, −3 the worst, and Rainbow is a special top tier for the rarest passives, and the 1.0 launch added an even rarer World Tree tier — high-power passives with built-in trade-offs, found only on Pals caught in the World Tree region. A Pal's passives are hidden until you capture it, and you can't change them afterwards — but children inherit passives from their parents, so chain-breeding is how you build a "perfect" Pal (e.g. Legend + Ferocious + Musclehead + Demon God for raw damage, or Artisan + Remarkable Craftsmanship + Serious + Work Slave for a base worker). Boss-exclusive passives like Legend or Invader must first be caught on their source Pal before they can be bred onto anything else.

How the passive builds work

Recommended builds follow four archetypes. Attackers stack Legend + Demon God + Ferocious + their matching element passive (e.g. Divine Dragon on a Dragon type) — swap in Musclehead if the Pal never works at base. Workers want Remarkable Craftsmanship + Artisan + Serious + a fourth slot (Work Slave for max speed if it never fights, or Nocturnal/Vampiric so it works through the night). Mounts stack Swift + Runner + Nimble + a stamina passive (Eternal Engine > Infinite Stamina). Ranch Pals don't benefit from work speed — keep them fed and sane with Diet Lover, Mastery of Fasting, Workaholic and Positive Thinker instead. Hover any passive chip to see what it does.

How work suitability levels work

Every Pal has one or more work suitabilities, each with a level. Higher level = faster work, and some structures demand a minimum level just to be operated. Early Access topped out around Level 4 for most jobs — 1.0 raised the ceiling, with specialists like Solenne (Handiwork 8) and Renjishi (Kindling 8) redefining top-end bases; the suitability scale itself now runs toward Level 10. Levels can be raised further: Pal Condensation (48 copies for max rank since 1.0) upgrades a Pal's stars, some partner skills boost the whole base (Puffolt grants +1 Generating to every base Pal), and 1.0's mutation passives like Farmhand and Ranch Master add +1/+2 to specific jobs. One management tip above all: a multi-skill Pal wanders between tasks — use the Monitoring Stand to lock specialists to their specialty.

🔥

Kindling

Lights furnaces and cooking stations — smelting ingots, cooking food, firing kilns. Higher-tier furnaces need higher Kindling levels to operate at all.

Top workers: Renjishi (Lv8), Blazamut Ryu (Lv4), Jormuntide Ignis (Lv4)
💧

Watering

Waters crop plots, powers the crusher and mill, and keeps hydration-based stations running.

Top workers: Jormuntide (Lv4), Whalaska (Lv3), Shaolong (Lv3)
🌱

Planting

Sows seeds at berry, wheat and vegetable plantations — the start of every food and Cake pipeline.

Top workers: Lyleen (Lv4), Leafan, Petallia (Lv3)
⚡

Generating Electricity

Charges power generators that feed assembly lines, fridges and late-game machinery.

Top workers: Orserk (Lv4), Puffolt (+1 to every base Pal), Grizzbolt (Lv3)
✋

Handiwork

Crafting at workbenches, assembly lines, and construction of buildings — the busiest job in any production base.

Top workers: Solenne (Lv8), Anubis (Lv4), Wumpo Botan (Lv2)
🧺

Gathering

Harvests ripe crops and picks berries so your Planting Pals' work actually reaches the feed box.

Top workers: Frostallion Noct (Lv4), Verdash (Lv3), Nitewing (Lv2)
🪓

Lumbering

Chops trees and works the logging site for a steady wood supply.

Top workers: Wumpo Botan (Lv3), Warsect (Lv3), Bushi (Lv3)
⛏️

Mining

Breaks ore, stone and coal nodes and staffs the stone pit — plus 1.0's Soralite Quarry automation.

Top workers: Astegon (Lv4), Blazamut (Lv4), Digtoise (Lv3)
💊

Medicine Production

Brews medicines and the Remedies/Elixirs that permanently boost your stats at the Medieval Medicine Workbench.

Top workers: Lyleen (Lv3), Lyleen Noct (Lv3), Petallia (Lv2)
❄️

Cooling

Runs coolers and fridges to stop food spoiling, and powers ice-based production stations.

Top workers: Frostallion (Lv4), Bastigor (Lv3), Whalaska (Lv2)
📦

Transporting

Hauls crafted goods and gathered materials into storage — the invisible job that stops a base silting up.

Top workers: Wumpo Botan (Lv4), Faleris (Lv3), Helzephyr (Lv3)
🐑

Farming

Ranch production: assigned Pals generate goods over time — eggs, milk, wool, honey, even gold coins.

Top workers: Chikipi (eggs), Mozzarina (milk), Beegarde (honey), Mau (gold)
