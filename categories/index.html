How pages link together (the architecture)

The wiki follows a hub-and-spoke rule set so nothing is ever a dead end. Hubs are category pages like this one — they only contain links down to entries. Entries (a Pal, an item, a boss) always link sideways to related entries and up to their hubs. The standing rules: every Pal links to breeding (its card links to the Breeding guide today; when per-Pal pages exist, that becomes its exact parent combinations and what it breeds into); every item links to its crafting recipe and every recipe links back to each ingredient; every resource links to gathering locations and the tools/Pals that harvest it; every boss links to its drops and a strategy section, and each drop links onward to what it crafts. Passives are already the model: a Pal card names its passives → hover shows the effect and source → the source names a boss or system → which is searchable one tab away. Deep links (e.g. /?tab=pals&role=fly) make every filtered view shareable, so guide pages can cite "all Flying Pals" as a real link rather than a phrase. The test for good discoverability: from any page, any related fact should be at most two clicks away.
